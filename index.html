ExtraGS: Extrapolated Street View Synthesis
with Geometric and Generative Prior

Abstract

Synthesizing realistic extrapolated views from recorded driving logs is crucial for realistic driving scene simulation, yet it remains a challenging task. Recent methods incorporate generative priors as pseudo ground truth at extrapolated views. However, naively exploiting these generative priors often result in degradation of performance in the original trajectory and results in lack of geometric consistency. We propose ExtraGS, a holistic approach for driving scene trajectory extrapolation that combines geometric and generative priors. Central to our method is a road surface model that employs a dimension-reduced signed distance function to precisely capture road surface geometries. Additionally, ExtraGS incorporates a novel self-supervised uncertainty estimation technique, providing precise control to information from pretrained generative models. Comprehensive experiments across various datasets, diverse multi-camera configurations, and multiple genera- tive prior selections demonstrate that ExtraGS substantially improves the realism and consistency of extrapolated views, while maintaining strong performance on original trajectories.

Pipeline

(a) Our method decomposes driving scenes into specialized components based on their distinct geometric properties. (b) We define a self-supervised uncertainty estimation method, which models how likely is a view from the training data. The uncertainty is used to control the information from the generative model by scaling the gradient from the the loss functions from the pseudo ground truth. The Directional Deviation can also be rendered together with rgb values to serve as an inpainting mask to guide the diffusion process. (c) We leverage pretrained multi-view generative model in a zero-shot manner. Extra conditions such as 3D object bounding boxes and text descriptions are provided as additional guidance.
